Lake Markakol

Lake Markakol is an amazingly beautiful place in Eastern Kazakhstan, known for its incredible landscapes, especially during sunset or dawn. It gained wide popularity and recognition among travelers thanks to its crystal-clear water. The lake is located in the nature reserve of the same name. The landscapes that surround Markakol are diverse: there are meadows, mountains, taiga, where more than 700 plant species grow. Brown bears, snow leopards, wolverines, wild boars, wolves, moose and other amazing animals are found here. Winter in this region is very harsh, but in the summer it is always warm and sunny here.With a charming landscape, Lake Markakol will charm you at first sight. Located 1,447 meters above the sea level, the ellipse-shaped lake is surrounded by the Kurchum and Azutau mountain ranges, measuring a length of nearly 40 kilometers. The lake turns bright blue when the sun is shining, and appears in a gray-black or silvery color when the clouds cover the sky. In 1976, the government of then Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic announced the creation of a nature reserve around the territory of the lake. Today tourists visit Lake Markakol to enjoy pristine nature and see one of the most beautiful high-mountainous lakes within the entirety of Central Asia.
